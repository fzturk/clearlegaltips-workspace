## Online Divorce in Texas (Complete Guide)

### Texas Requirements

- **Residency:** 6 months in Texas + 90 days in filing county
- **Grounds:** "Insupportability" (no-fault) — no specific reason needed
- **Waiting period:** 60 days from filing date
- **Division standard:** Community property state (50/50 presumption)

### Texas Online Divorce Process

**Step 1: File Original Petition for Divorce**
- File with the District Clerk in your county
- E-filing available in ALL Texas counties (mandatory in most)
- Filing fee: $250-$350 (varies by county)

**Step 2: Serve Your Spouse**
- **Waiver of Citation:** Spouse signs form accepting service (fastest — $0)
- **Personal service:** Constable or process server ($75-$150)
- **Service by posting/publication:** Only if spouse cannot be located

**Step 3: 60-Day Cooling Period**
- Court cannot finalize until 60 days after filing
- Use this time to draft your Final Decree
- No action required — just wait

**Step 4: Draft Final Decree of Divorce**
- Marital Settlement Agreement (property/debt division)
- Child custody order and possession schedule (if children)
- Child support calculation (Texas uses percentage guidelines)

**Step 5: Prove-Up Hearing**
- Brief hearing (10-20 minutes) where judge approves your agreement
- Only the filing spouse typically needs to appear
- Some Texas counties allow "prove-up by affidavit" (no appearance)

**Step 6: Judge Signs Final Decree**
- Divorce is final when judge signs
- Minimum timeline: 61 days from filing

### Texas-Specific Notes

- **Community property:** All property acquired during marriage is presumed 50/50
- **Standard Possession Order:** Default visitation schedule for non-custodial parent
- **No spousal support for marriages under 10 years** (with limited exceptions)
- **Texas child support:** % of net income (20% for 1 child, 25% for 2, etc.)
- **Informal marriages (common law):** Same divorce process required

## Online Divorce in California (Complete Guide)

### California Requirements

- **Residency:** 6 months in California + 3 months in filing county
- **Grounds:** "Irreconcilable differences" (no-fault only)
- **Waiting period:** 6 months from date of service (longest in US)
- **Division standard:** Community property state (50/50)

### California Online Divorce Process

**Step 1: File Petition (FL-100)**
- File with Superior Court in your county
- E-filing available in most California counties
- Filing fee: $435-$450
- **Fee waiver available** if income below 125% of poverty level

**Step 2: Serve Your Spouse**
- Cannot serve yourself — must use third party (18+ years old)
- **Notice and Acknowledgment of Receipt:** Spouse signs form via mail ($0)
- **Personal service:** Process server ($50-$150)
- **Service deadline:** Serve within 60 days of filing

**Step 3: Spouse Files Response (FL-120) — Or Not**
- **Spouse responds:** Both negotiate terms → settlement agreement
- **Spouse doesn't respond (30 days):** Default divorce — your terms are accepted
- Response filing fee: $435

**Step 4: Financial Disclosures (MANDATORY)**
- Both parties must exchange Preliminary Declaration of Disclosure (FL-140)
- Income and Expense Declaration (FL-150)
- Schedule of Assets and Debts (FL-142)
- **Failure to exchange = divorce cannot be finalized**

**Step 5: Marital Settlement Agreement (FL-180)**
- If you agree on all terms, file your MSA with the court
- Judge reviews for fairness
- No hearing required for uncontested cases

**Step 6: Judgment Entered After 6-Month Wait**
- Divorce is final on Status Only basis
- Can request bifurcation (early termination of marriage status) in complex cases

### California-Specific Notes

- **6-month waiting period** starts from DATE OF SERVICE (not filing date)
- **Summary dissolution:** Simplified process for short marriages with limited assets:
  - Married less than 5 years
  - No children
  - No real property
  - Less than $47,000 in community property debts
  - Less than $47,000 in community property assets (excluding cars)
  - Neither party owns more than $47,000 in separate property
- **Pension/retirement:** QDRO required for division (additional $500-$1,500 cost)
- **Registered Domestic Partners:** Same process as divorce

## Online Divorce in Florida (Complete Guide)

### Florida Requirements

- **Residency:** 6 months in Florida (either spouse)
- **Grounds:** "Irretrievably broken" (no-fault)
- **Waiting period:** 20 days from filing (shortest of the three)
- **Division standard:** Equitable distribution (fair, not necessarily 50/50)

### Florida Online Divorce Process

**Step 1: File Petition for Dissolution of Marriage**
- File with Circuit Court Clerk in your county
- E-filing available statewide (Florida Courts E-Filing Portal)
- Filing fee: $400-$410
- **Simplified dissolution** available if you qualify

**Step 2: Serve Your Spouse**
- **Answer and Waiver:** Spouse files a waiver of service + answer ($0 service cost)
- **Personal service:** Sheriff or process server ($30-$75)
- **Constructive service:** By publication if spouse cannot be found

**Step 3: Financial Disclosures**
- Both parties file Financial Affidavit (Form 12.902)
  - **Short form:** If income under $50,000/year
  - **Long form:** If income $50,000+/year
- Must be filed within 45 days of service

**Step 4: Parenting Course (If Children)**
- **Mandatory** for all divorces involving minor children
- 4-hour approved parent education course
- Cost: $25-$75 (available online)
- Both parents must complete before finalization

**Step 5: Marital Settlement Agreement**
- Property division agreement
- Parenting Plan (if children) — Florida uses "time-sharing" not "custody"
- Child support guidelines calculation

**Step 6: Final Hearing**
- **Simplified dissolution:** Brief hearing, both parties present, no attorney needed
- **Regular uncontested:** Brief hearing (10-15 minutes), petitioner appears
- **Some counties:** Allow final judgment without hearing for fully agreed cases

**Step 7: Final Judgment**
- Judge signs Final Judgment of Dissolution
- Effective immediately (20-day wait has passed)
- Cannot remarry for 30 days after judgment in some counties (verify locally)

### Florida-Specific Notes

- **Simplified dissolution** available if:
  - Both parties agree on ALL terms
  - No minor children
  - Wife is not pregnant
  - At least one party has lived in Florida 6+ months
  - Both parties attend final hearing
- **Equitable distribution:** NOT automatic 50/50 — judge considers factors including length of marriage, economic circumstances, contributions
- **"Time-sharing":** Florida eliminated "custody" and "visitation" terminology in 2008
- **Alimony reform (2023):** No permanent alimony for marriages under 20 years; durational limits apply
- **Mandatory disclosure:** 45-day deadline is strictly enforced

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Which state is cheapest for online divorce?
Texas has the lowest filing fees ($250-$350) and shortest practical timeline (61 days). Florida has the shortest waiting period (20 days) but higher filing fees ($400+). California is most expensive with the longest mandatory wait (6 months).

### Can I file in a different state than where I live?
No — you must meet the residency requirement of the state where you file. You cannot "shop" for a cheaper or faster state.

### What if my spouse lives in a different state?
You can still file in YOUR state of residence. You'll serve your spouse in their state. This can complicate things — especially for property in other states.

### Do I need to appear in court?
- **Texas:** Usually yes (brief prove-up hearing), but some counties allow affidavit
- **California:** No (for uncontested — judge reviews paperwork)
- **Florida:** Yes (final hearing required, but brief)

### Can I do my own divorce completely free?
Yes — all three states offer free self-help forms on their court websites. You'll still pay the court filing fee, but you can avoid the $299 service fee if you're comfortable researching and completing the forms yourself.
