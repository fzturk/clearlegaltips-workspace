## Step 1: Choose Your LLC Name (2 Minutes)

### Name Requirements (All States):
- Must include "LLC," "L.L.C.," or "Limited Liability Company"
- Cannot include words implying it's a different entity type (Inc., Corp.)
- Cannot include restricted words without permission (Bank, Insurance, University)
- Must be distinguishable from existing registered entities in your state

### Check Name Availability:
1. Search your state's Secretary of State business name database (free)
2. Check for trademark conflicts at the USPTO database
3. Verify the domain name is available

### Naming Tips:
- Keep it broad enough to cover future business directions
- Avoid names too similar to established brands
- Consider how it looks on business cards, invoices, and signage
- Don't include the owner's name if you may sell the business later

## Step 2: Choose Your State (1 Minute)

**For 95% of small businesses: form in your HOME state.** 

Only consider another state if:
- You plan to raise venture capital (Delaware)
- You have specific asset protection needs (Wyoming)
- You operate equally in multiple states with no primary location

Forming out-of-state while operating in your home state means paying fees in BOTH states.

## Step 4: File Articles of Organization (5 Minutes)

This is the actual formation step. You can file:

### Option A: Direct with Secretary of State
1. Go to your state's Secretary of State website
2. Find the LLC formation/filing section
3. Complete the online form:
   - LLC name
   - Registered agent name and address
   - Principal office address
   - Member/Manager names (some states)
   - Organizer's name and signature
   - Business purpose (some states)
4. Pay the filing fee (credit card)
5. Receive confirmation/tracking number

**Processing time:** Same-day to 4 weeks (varies by state)

## Step 6: Get Your EIN — Free from IRS (5 Minutes)

After your LLC is officially formed:
1. Visit the IRS EIN Online Assistant
2. Select "Limited Liability Company"
3. Enter your LLC information
4. Receive your EIN instantly on screen
5. Print the confirmation — you'll need it for banking

## Common LLC Formation Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Not Getting an Operating Agreement
Banks require it. Courts look for it when evaluating liability protection. Single-member LLCs especially need one to prove the LLC is a separate entity from the owner.

### Mistake 2: Commingling Personal and Business Funds
Using your personal checking account for business expenses can "pierce the corporate veil" — eliminating your liability protection. Open a separate business bank account immediately.

### Mistake 3: Forgetting Annual Compliance
Missing annual reports can result in administrative dissolution. Set calendar reminders or use a compliance service.

### Mistake 4: Choosing the Wrong State
Forming in Delaware or Wyoming while operating in California means paying fees in BOTH states. Form where you operate.

### Mistake 5: Paying for Free Services
EINs are free from the IRS. Don't pay $50-$150 for a service to submit the same free form.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does it take to form an LLC?

Filing time: 10-30 minutes. State processing: same-day to 4 weeks depending on the state. With expedited processing: 1-3 business days in most states.

### Do I need a lawyer to form an LLC?

No. LLC formation is straightforward for most single-member and simple multi-member LLCs. Online formation services or direct state filing work perfectly. Attorneys are recommended for complex ownership structures, multiple classes of membership, or significant capital.

### Can I form an LLC by myself as the only member?

Yes — single-member LLCs are the most common type. You are the sole owner, manager, and can be your own registered agent. All 50 states allow single-member LLCs.

### What's the difference between member-managed and manager-managed?

Member-managed: all owners participate in daily business decisions (most common for small LLCs). Manager-managed: designated managers run the business; other members are passive investors.

### Do I need a business address that's different from my home?

No — your home address is perfectly acceptable for LLC formation in all states. However, it becomes public record. If privacy matters, use a registered agent service address.
