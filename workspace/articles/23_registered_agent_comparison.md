## What Does a Registered Agent Do?

A registered agent performs three critical functions:

### 1. Receives Legal Documents (Service of Process)
If your LLC is sued, the plaintiff serves legal papers through your registered agent. Missing a lawsuit notice because no one was at the address can result in a default judgment against your business.

### 2. Accepts Government Correspondence
State filings, tax notices, annual report reminders, compliance notifications — all official state communications go through your registered agent address.

### 3. Maintains a Physical Presence
Your registered agent must be available at a physical street address (not a P.O. Box) during normal business hours in the state of formation. This ensures the state and courts can always reach your business.

## State-Specific Registered Agent Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Physical address | Must be a street address in the state (no P.O. Box) |
| Availability | During normal business hours |
| Person or entity | Individual (18+) or authorized business entity |
| Same state | Must be physically located in the state of formation |
| Foreign LLCs | Need an agent in EACH state where registered |

### States With Special Rules

**California:** Agent must file a Statement of Information within 90 days of LLC formation (every 2 years after). Agent address goes on this public filing.

**New York:** Agent designated in Articles of Organization. Address used for service of process — critical for the publication requirement.

**Delaware:** Many businesses form in Delaware but operate elsewhere. You MUST have a Delaware registered agent if formed there, plus agents in each state where you do business.

## How to Change Your Registered Agent

Already have an agent and want to switch? The process is simple in most states:

1. Sign up with new registered agent service
2. File a "Change of Registered Agent" form with the Secretary of State ($0-$25 fee)
3. New agent takes effect upon filing
4. Cancel your old service after confirmation
5. Update any third parties who send correspondence to the old agent

Most registered agent services handle the change-of-agent filing for you at no extra cost.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What happens if I don't have a registered agent?

Your state can revoke or administratively dissolve your LLC for failing to maintain a registered agent. You also risk missing critical legal documents — if someone sues your business and you don't receive the papers, the court can enter a default judgment against you without your knowledge.

### Can I use a P.O. Box as my registered agent address?

No — every state requires a physical street address. P.O. Boxes, virtual offices without physical presence, and mail forwarding addresses don't qualify. The address must be a location where someone is physically present during business hours.

### Can my accountant or lawyer be my registered agent?

Yes — any individual with a physical address in your state can serve. Attorneys and accountants commonly serve as registered agents for their clients. However, they may charge comparable fees to a dedicated service ($100-$300/year).

### Do I need a registered agent in every state where I do business?

Yes — if your LLC is registered (domestically or as a foreign LLC) in multiple states, you need a registered agent in EACH state. This is where multi-state discount pricing from services like Northwest ($100/state) provides significant savings.

### How quickly will I be notified of legal documents?

Professional services typically scan and email documents the same business day they're received. Some offer instant notification. Self-serving means you see the document when the process server hands it to you (or leaves it at your door).
