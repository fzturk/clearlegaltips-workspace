## Probate Cost Estimator — By State (2026)

Probate is expensive. Between court fees, attorney fees, executor compensation, and appraisal costs, probate typically consumes 3-7% of the estate's total value. On a $500,000 estate, that's $15,000-$35,000 in costs that could have gone to your heirs.

## Attorney Fees in Probate — How They're Calculated

### Statutory Fees (States That Set Fees by Law)

**California** and several other states set attorney fees as a percentage of the estate's gross value:

| Estate Value | CA Attorney Fee | CA Executor Fee | Combined |
|:-----------:|:--------------:|:--------------:|:--------:|
| First $100,000 | 4% ($4,000) | 4% ($4,000) | $8,000 |
| Next $100,000 | 3% ($3,000) | 3% ($3,000) | $6,000 |
| Next $800,000 | 2% ($16,000) | 2% ($16,000) | $32,000 |
| Next $9,000,000 | 1% | 1% | 2% |
| **$500,000 estate** | **$13,000** | **$13,000** | **$26,000** |
| **$1,000,000 estate** | **$23,000** | **$23,000** | **$46,000** |

**Important:** California fees are based on GROSS estate value (before debts). A home worth $800,000 with a $400,000 mortgage has fees calculated on $800,000 — not the $400,000 equity.

### Reasonable Fee States (Most States)
Most states allow "reasonable" attorney fees without a statutory formula. Typical ranges:
- Simple estate: $2,000-$5,000
- Moderate estate: $5,000-$15,000
- Complex estate: $15,000-$50,000+

### Hourly Rate States
Some estates pay attorneys hourly:
- Small-town attorney: $200-$350/hour
- Urban probate attorney: $350-$600/hour
- Total hours (simple estate): 15-30 hours
- Total hours (complex estate): 50-200+ hours

**Can an executor waive their fee?** Yes — family members serving as executor often waive compensation, especially when they're also a beneficiary. Waiving the fee means the amount isn't taxable income to the executor.

## How to Avoid Probate (and Its Costs)

### Method 1: Revocable Living Trust
Transfer assets to a trust during your lifetime. At death, the successor trustee distributes assets per trust instructions — no court involvement, no probate fees, no public record.

**Cost to create:** $1,000-$3,000 (attorney) or $0-$200 (template/online)
**Savings:** Avoids 3-7% probate costs on all trust assets

[THIRSTY: LegalZoom Estate Planning]

### Method 2: Beneficiary Designations
Retirement accounts, life insurance, and POD/TOD bank accounts pass directly to named beneficiaries — bypassing probate entirely.

### Method 3: Joint Ownership
Property owned as "joint tenants with right of survivorship" passes automatically to the surviving owner at death.

### Method 4: Transfer-on-Death Deeds
Available in 29+ states. Register a TOD deed for real property — it transfers automatically at death without probate.

### Method 5: Small Estate Procedures
If the estate is below your state's simplified probate threshold (see table above), you may avoid full probate through an affidavit process or summary administration.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does probate take?

Simple estates: 6-12 months. Moderate estates: 12-18 months. Complex or contested estates: 2-5+ years. During this time, heirs generally cannot access estate assets (except for approved family allowances).

### Can I avoid probate completely?

Yes — through a combination of living trust, beneficiary designations, joint ownership, and TOD deeds, you can ensure no assets require probate. Comprehensive estate planning is the key.

### Who pays probate costs — the heirs or the estate?

The estate pays all probate costs before any distribution to heirs. This reduces what the heirs receive.

### Is probate public record?

Yes — probate filings (including the will, asset inventory, and creditor claims) are public record. Anyone can access them. This is one reason high-profile individuals use trusts instead.

### Do all assets go through probate?

No. Assets that bypass probate include: trust assets, beneficiary-designated accounts (retirement, life insurance), jointly-owned property with survivorship rights, and assets with TOD/POD designations.
