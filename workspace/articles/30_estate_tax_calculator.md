## Estate Tax Threshold Calculator — Federal & State (2026)

Will your estate owe taxes when you die? For most Americans, the answer is no — the federal estate tax exemption is $13.99 million per individual in 2026. But 12 states plus DC impose their own estate taxes with much lower thresholds (as low as $1 million). Use our tables below to determine your exposure.

**What this means:** If your total estate (assets minus debts) is below the exemption amount, you owe $0 in federal estate tax. Only the amount ABOVE the exemption is taxed at rates from 18% to 40%.

## Estate Tax Reduction Strategies

### Strategy 1: Use Your Annual Gift Exclusion
Give up to $18,000 per person per year (2025, indexed) without affecting your estate tax exemption. A couple can give $36,000 per recipient per year. Over 20 years, a couple with 3 children and 6 grandchildren can transfer $6.48 million tax-free through annual gifts alone.

### Strategy 2: Irrevocable Life Insurance Trust (ILIT)
Transfer life insurance ownership to an irrevocable trust. The death benefit is removed from your taxable estate. A $2 million policy owned by you = $2 million added to your estate. Same policy in an ILIT = $0 added.

### Strategy 3: Spousal Portability Election
When the first spouse dies, file an estate tax return (Form 706) to "port" their unused exemption to the surviving spouse. This effectively gives the surviving spouse up to $27.98 million in combined exemption (2025).

### Strategy 4: Charitable Giving
Assets left to qualified charities are fully deductible from the taxable estate. A Charitable Remainder Trust (CRT) provides income during life and removes assets from your estate at death.

### Strategy 5: Family Limited Partnership (FLP)
Transfer business or investment assets to an FLP. Limited partnership interests can be gifted at a valuation discount (25-40% typical) due to lack of marketability and minority interest.

### Strategy 6: Revocable Living Trust (For State Tax Planning)
While a revocable trust doesn't reduce federal estate taxes, it avoids probate — saving 2-7% in executor fees and court costs. For residents of estate tax states, trust planning can incorporate strategies to minimize state exposure.

## The 2026 Sunset: What You Need to Know

The Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA) of 2017 doubled the federal estate tax exemption from approximately $5.5 million to $11+ million (now $13.99 million with inflation adjustments). This provision is scheduled to "sunset" on December 31, 2025.

**If Congress does nothing:** The exemption drops to approximately $7 million per person in 2026.
**If Congress extends:** The exemption continues at $14+ million.

### Planning for Uncertainty

- **Estates under $7 million:** Likely safe regardless of what happens
- **Estates between $7 million and $14 million:** Exposure ONLY if the law sunsets. Consider making gifts before 2026 to lock in the higher exemption
- **Estates over $14 million:** Subject to federal estate tax regardless. Advanced planning recommended

**IRS has confirmed:** Gifts made under the higher exemption (pre-2026) will NOT be "clawed back" if the exemption later decreases. This is the "use it or lose it" planning opportunity.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does everyone pay estate tax?

No — approximately 99.5% of estates owe zero federal estate tax. The current exemption ($13.99 million in 2025) means only estates above that threshold pay anything. However, residents of states with lower exemptions (Oregon at $1 million, Massachusetts at $2 million) face state estate tax at much lower wealth levels.

### Is life insurance included in my taxable estate?

Yes — if YOU own the policy. The full death benefit is included in your estate. To remove it, transfer ownership to an Irrevocable Life Insurance Trust (ILIT) or have another person own the policy. There's a 3-year lookback period — transfers within 3 years of death are still included.

### What's the difference between estate tax and inheritance tax?

Estate tax is paid by the deceased person's estate before distribution. Inheritance tax is paid by the person who receives the inheritance. Six states have inheritance tax; rates depend on the heir's relationship to the deceased (spouses and children usually exempt or pay low rates).

### Can I avoid estate tax by putting everything in a trust?

A revocable living trust does NOT reduce estate tax — you still control the assets, so they're still "yours" for tax purposes. An irrevocable trust CAN remove assets from your estate, but you permanently give up control. Different trust types serve different purposes.

### Should I worry about estate tax if I'm not wealthy?

At the federal level, probably not (unless the 2026 sunset occurs and your estate is above $7 million). At the state level, if you live in Oregon ($1M threshold) or Massachusetts ($2M threshold), homeowners with retirement accounts can easily exceed these amounts. A home worth $800,000 + $500,000 in retirement accounts + $200,000 in other assets = $1.5 million — enough to trigger Oregon estate tax.
