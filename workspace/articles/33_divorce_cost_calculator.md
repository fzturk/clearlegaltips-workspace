## How to Minimize Divorce Costs

### 1. Agree on Everything Before Filing
The single biggest cost driver is disagreement. Couples who agree on property division, custody, and support before filing spend 80-90% less than those who litigate.

### 2. Use an Online Divorce Service
For uncontested divorces, online services prepare all paperwork for $149-$499 — a fraction of attorney costs.

### 3. Try Mediation Before Litigation
A mediator ($100-$300/hour, 5-10 sessions) costs $1,000-$5,000 total. Compare that to two attorneys at $300-$500/hour for months of litigation.

### 4. Use a Collaborative Divorce Process
Each spouse hires a collaborative attorney who commits to resolving issues without court. If it fails, both attorneys must withdraw — creating strong incentive to settle.

### 5. Limit Attorney Scope
Hire an attorney for specific tasks (document review, complex asset questions) rather than full representation. "Unbundled" legal services cost $500-$2,000 vs. $10,000+ for full representation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the cheapest way to get a divorce?

Uncontested DIY divorce: agree on all terms, prepare paperwork yourself (or use an online service for $149-$499), file with the court, and attend the final hearing. Total cost: state filing fee + service of process = $300-$750 in most states.

### Can I get a divorce without a lawyer?

Yes — especially for uncontested divorces. Most states allow pro se (self-represented) filing. Online services like CompleteCase prepare all your documents for $299. You still need to file with the court and may need to attend a hearing.

### How long does a divorce take?

Uncontested: 2-6 months (plus any mandatory waiting period). Contested: 8-24 months. High-conflict with custody disputes: 1-3+ years. Mandatory waiting periods (30-365 days depending on state) apply regardless.

### Who pays for the divorce?

Each spouse typically pays their own attorney fees. The filing fee is paid by the petitioner (spouse who files). Courts can order one spouse to pay the other's fees if there's a significant income disparity.

### Does it cost more to get divorced if we have children?

Yes — custody arrangements, child support calculations, parenting plans, and potentially guardian ad litem fees add $2,000-$30,000+ depending on whether you agree on custody terms.
