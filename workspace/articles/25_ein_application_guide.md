## What Is an EIN and Who Needs One?

An EIN (also called a Federal Tax Identification Number or FEIN) is essentially a Social Security Number for your business. It's used on tax returns, bank accounts, business applications, and any context where your business needs to be identified by the IRS.

### You MUST Have an EIN If:

- You have employees (current or planned)
- Your business is taxed as a corporation or partnership
- You file employment, excise, or alcohol/tobacco/firearms tax returns
- You withhold taxes on income paid to a non-resident alien
- You have a Keogh retirement plan
- You're involved with trusts, estates, real estate investment conduits, or nonprofits

### You SHOULD Get an EIN Even If Not Required:

- **Single-member LLC:** Technically can use your SSN, but an EIN prevents giving your SSN to every vendor, client, and bank
- **Sole proprietor with no employees:** Same privacy benefit — EIN on invoices and W-9s instead of SSN
- **Opening a business bank account:** Most banks require an EIN for business accounts
- **Applying for business credit:** Establishes your business as a separate entity from you

## How to Get an EIN for Free (IRS Direct - 4 Methods)

### Method 1: Online Application (Recommended — Instant)

**Cost: $0 | Time: 5-10 minutes | Available: Mon-Fri, 7AM-10PM Eastern**

The IRS EIN Online Assistant is the fastest method. You answer a few questions and receive your EIN immediately upon completion.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Go to the IRS EIN application page (search "IRS EIN online application")
2. Click "Apply Online Now"
3. Select your entity type (LLC, Corporation, Partnership, Sole Proprietor, etc.)
4. Enter the responsible party's information (name, SSN, address)
5. Describe your business (type, reason for applying, start date)
6. Indicate number of employees expected in the next 12 months
7. Review and submit
8. **Receive your EIN immediately on screen — print or save the confirmation**

**Requirements:**
- Responsible party must have a valid SSN or ITIN
- One EIN per responsible party per day (daily limit)
- Must complete in one session (no saving progress)
- US-based applicants only (international applicants use fax or mail)

### Method 2: Fax Application (Form SS-4)

**Cost: $0 | Time: 4 business days**

1. Download and complete IRS Form SS-4
2. Fax to the IRS at the appropriate number for your state
3. Receive your EIN by return fax within 4 business days

**Best for:** International applicants, applicants who prefer paper forms

### Method 3: Mail Application (Form SS-4)

**Cost: $0 | Time: 4-6 weeks**

1. Complete Form SS-4
2. Mail to the appropriate IRS address
3. Receive EIN by mail

**Best for:** Only if online and fax are not available

### Method 4: Phone Application

**Cost: $0 | Time: Varies (hold times can be long)**

1. Call the IRS Business & Specialty Tax Line
2. Have Form SS-4 completed and ready to reference
3. An agent processes your application over the phone

**Best for:** International applicants (dedicated international line available)

## EIN Application: Common Questions and Pitfalls

### What Information You'll Need

| Information | Details |
|------------|---------|
| Entity type | LLC, Corporation, Partnership, Sole Proprietor, Trust, Estate, etc. |
| Legal name | Exact entity name as registered with the state |
| Trade name / DBA | If different from legal name |
| Responsible party | Individual who controls/manages the entity (name + SSN) |
| Business address | Physical street address (P.O. Box accepted for mailing) |
| State of formation | Where your LLC/Corp was formed |
| Business start date | When the entity was formed or began operations |
| Closing month | Fiscal year end month (December for most) |
| Number of employees | Expected in the next 12 months |
| Reason for applying | Started new business, hired employees, banking, etc. |

### The "Responsible Party" Requirement

The IRS requires one individual as the "responsible party" — the person who controls, manages, or directs the entity. This person's SSN is used to validate the EIN application.

For LLCs: Usually the sole member or managing member
For Corporations: Usually an officer (President, CEO)
For Partnerships: Usually a general partner

### After You Get Your EIN

1. **Save your confirmation letter** — The IRS mails a CP 575 confirmation letter. If you applied online, print/save the confirmation page.
2. **Open a business bank account** — Bring your EIN confirmation and formation documents
3. **Give your EIN (not SSN) to clients and vendors** — Use on W-9 forms
4. **File your first tax return** using the EIN
5. **Register for state tax accounts** — Sales tax, employer withholding (if applicable)

## EIN vs. SSN vs. ITIN — What's the Difference?

| Number | Who Gets It | Purpose | Format |
|--------|-----------|---------|--------|
| **SSN** | US citizens and authorized workers | Personal tax ID | XXX-XX-XXXX |
| **EIN** | Businesses and entities | Business tax ID | XX-XXXXXXX |
| **ITIN** | Non-residents with US tax obligations | Individual tax ID (no SSN) | 9XX-XX-XXXX |

**Can I use my SSN instead of an EIN?**
- Sole proprietors with no employees: yes (but not recommended for privacy)
- Single-member LLCs with no employees: yes (but EIN preferred)
- Partnerships, corporations, multi-member LLCs: no — EIN required

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is an EIN really free?

Yes — 100% free from the IRS. There is no fee to apply for, receive, or use an EIN. Any website charging you for an EIN is charging for their service of submitting the free application on your behalf, not for the EIN itself.

### How long does it take to get an EIN?

Online: instant (you receive it on screen when you complete the application). Fax: 4 business days. Mail: 4-6 weeks. Phone: during the call (if you reach an agent).

### Can I apply for an EIN before forming my LLC?

No — you need the entity's legal name and formation date. Apply for the EIN after your LLC or corporation is officially formed with the state. Sole proprietors can apply at any time.

### Do I need a new EIN if I change my LLC's name?

Generally no — a name change doesn't require a new EIN. However, if you change your entity type (sole proprietor to LLC, partnership to corporation, etc.), you typically need a new EIN.

### Can I have more than one EIN?

Each entity gets one EIN. But you can have multiple EINs if you own multiple businesses (each LLC or corporation gets its own). You cannot have multiple EINs for the same entity.

### What if I lose my EIN?

Check previous tax returns (it's on every return you've filed). Call the IRS Business & Specialty Tax Line with your entity information — they can look it up. Check your bank account opening documents. Search your email for the online confirmation.
