## How to File a Trademark Online at the USPTO (2026)

Filing a trademark directly with the USPTO costs $250-$350 per class. The process takes 8-14 months from filing to registration, but the actual application takes about 30-60 minutes to complete online. Here's exactly how to do it.

## Step-by-Step USPTO Filing Process

### Step 1: Search for Conflicts (15 minutes)
Search the USPTO's TESS (Trademark Electronic Search System) database for existing registrations similar to your mark in the same goods/services classes.

### Step 2: Identify Your Class(es)
The Nice Classification system has 45 classes. Common ones:
- Class 9: Software, apps, electronics
- Class 25: Clothing
- Class 35: Business services, advertising, retail
- Class 41: Education, entertainment, coaching
- Class 42: Technology services, web development
- Class 43: Restaurant and food services

### Step 3: Choose Your Filing Option
**TEAS Plus ($250/class):** Must select goods/services descriptions from the USPTO pre-approved list. Cheaper but less flexible.

**TEAS Standard ($350/class):** Can write custom goods/services descriptions. More expensive but allows precision.

### Step 4: Complete the Application
1. Go to the USPTO's TEAS filing system
2. Select "New Application"
3. Choose TEAS Plus or TEAS Standard
4. Enter mark information (word mark text OR upload logo)
5. Enter owner/applicant information
6. Identify goods/services and class(es)
7. Select filing basis (Section 1a: use in commerce OR Section 1b: intent to use)
8. Upload specimen (if use-based): photo of mark on products, website screenshot, packaging
9. Sign the declaration
10. Pay the fee and submit

### Step 5: Monitor Your Application
After filing:
- 3-4 months: Examining attorney reviews
- If Office Action issued: you have 3 months to respond
- 8-12 months: Published for opposition (30-day period)
- 9-14 months: Registration certificate issued (if no opposition)

## Common Filing Mistakes to Avoid

1. **No search before filing** — Filing a conflicting mark wastes your $250-$350 fee
2. **Wrong filing basis** — Use-based requires a specimen; intent-to-use requires later Statement of Use
3. **Too broad descriptions** — Examiner refuses; you narrow anyway
4. **Wrong mark format** — Word mark = text only (broadest); design mark = specific logo only
5. **Missing specimen** — Must show actual use in commerce (not a mockup)
6. **Ignoring Office Actions** — 3-month deadline; miss it = application abandoned

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does trademark registration take?
8-14 months for straightforward applications. Add 3-6 months if you receive an Office Action. Total can be 12-18 months for complex applications.

### Can I use TM before registration?
Yes — TM (™) can be used immediately to indicate you're claiming trademark rights. The ® symbol can only be used AFTER official registration.

### What's a specimen of use?
Evidence showing your mark actually being used in commerce: product photos with the mark visible, website screenshots showing the mark with goods/services for sale, product packaging, labels, or tags.

### What if my application is refused?
You receive an Office Action explaining the refusal. Common reasons: likelihood of confusion with existing mark, mark is descriptive, specimen inadequate. You have 3 months to respond.
