## Living Trust vs Will — Complete Cost Comparison (2026)

Should you get a will or a living trust? The answer depends on your assets, family situation, and state. A will costs less upfront but may cost your heirs far more in probate. A trust costs more now but saves thousands (or tens of thousands) later.

Use our comparison below to make the right decision for your situation.

### When a Will Is Sufficient

- Estate under $100,000 (simplified probate available)
- All assets have beneficiary designations or joint ownership
- You're young with few assets and no real estate
- Single state, simple family situation
- You want court oversight of distribution

### When a Trust Is Worth the Cost

- Estate above $150,000 in probatable assets
- You own real estate (especially in multiple states)
- Privacy is important
- You want assets distributed quickly after death
- You want incapacity protection
- You have minor children (trust holds assets for them)
- Complex family (blended families, special needs beneficiaries)
- You own property in multiple states (avoids ancillary probate)

## The Multi-State Problem

If you own real property in more than one state, your heirs face probate in EACH state where property is located ("ancillary probate"). A living trust eliminates this entirely.

**Example:** You live in California and own a vacation home in Colorado.
- **With will:** Full probate in California + ancillary probate in Colorado = double fees, double timeline, double hassle
- **With trust:** Single trust administration, no court in either state

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I need both a will and a trust?

If you create a living trust, you still need a "pour-over will" — a simple will that catches any assets not transferred to the trust during your lifetime and "pours them over" into the trust at death. You also need a will to name guardians for minor children (trusts can't do this).

### Can I create a living trust without an attorney?

Yes — using a template or online service. However, the trust is only effective if properly "funded" (assets transferred into it). Improper funding is the #1 reason trusts fail to avoid probate. Attorney assistance with funding is highly recommended.

### How long does a living trust take to set up?

DIY/online: 1-3 hours for the document, plus days/weeks to fund assets. Attorney: 2-4 weeks for drafting, plus 1-4 weeks for funding (retitling assets into the trust name).

### What happens if my trust isn't funded at death?

Unfunded trust assets go through probate via the pour-over will — defeating the purpose of having a trust. The single most important step is transferring assets INTO the trust after creating it.

### Is a living trust better than a will for avoiding taxes?

A revocable living trust provides zero tax benefits during your lifetime — it's a probate-avoidance tool, not a tax-avoidance tool. For tax planning, you need irrevocable trusts, which are separate and more complex.
