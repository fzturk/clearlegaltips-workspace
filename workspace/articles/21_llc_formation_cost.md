## Hidden Costs to Watch For

### California's $800 Franchise Tax
California charges an $800 minimum franchise tax every year — regardless of whether your LLC earns any income. This applies starting your SECOND year (first year is exempt for new LLCs formed after 2020). A California LLC with zero revenue still owes $800/year to the Franchise Tax Board.

### New York's Publication Requirement
New York LLCs must publish formation notices in two newspapers (one daily, one weekly) in the county of formation for six consecutive weeks. Cost: $200-$2,000+ depending on the county. Manhattan and NYC counties are the most expensive. This is IN ADDITION to the $200 filing fee.

### Nevada's Business License Fee
Nevada advertises no state income tax, but charges a $200 annual business license fee PLUS the $150 annual report fee = $350/year minimum in state fees. Factor this in before forming in Nevada for "tax advantages."

### Annual Report Fees
Many entrepreneurs budget for formation but forget annual obligations. States like Florida ($138.75/year), North Carolina ($200/year), and Tennessee ($300 min/year) have substantial recurring fees.

### Registered Agent Renewal
Most online formation services include one year of registered agent service free, then charge $100-$299/year for renewal. Budget for this recurring cost.

## Should You Form in Your Home State or Another State?

### Form in Your Home State (Recommended for Most)

If you operate in one state, form there. Forming in Delaware or Wyoming while operating in California means:
- You pay Delaware/Wyoming fees AND California foreign qualification fees
- You need a registered agent in BOTH states
- You file annual reports in BOTH states
- You pay taxes in BOTH states (the state where you operate always taxes you)

**Total additional cost of forming out-of-state: $200-$600/year in duplicate fees**

### When Another State Makes Sense

- **Delaware:** If you plan to raise venture capital (VCs prefer Delaware law), or if you'll have complex multi-state operations
- **Wyoming:** Asset protection advantages (strong charging order protection); no state income tax on business income
- **Nevada:** No state income tax; privacy protections (no public member/manager listing)

**The honest math:** For 95% of small businesses, forming in your home state is cheaper and simpler than any out-of-state formation.

## How to Minimize LLC Formation Costs

### 1. Self-Designate as Registered Agent (Save $100-$299/year)
In most states, any adult with a physical address in the state can serve as registered agent. Use your home or office address. Downside: your address becomes public record, and you must be available during business hours.

### 2. File Directly with the State (Save $0-$399)
Every Secretary of State has an online filing portal. The forms are straightforward. If your LLC is simple (single member, no special provisions), you can file in under 30 minutes.

### 3. Use Our Free Operating Agreement Template (Save $500-$1,000)
An attorney-drafted operating agreement costs $500-$1,000. Our template covers single-member and multi-member LLCs with standard provisions.

### 4. Get Your EIN Free from the IRS (Save $50-$150)
Never pay a third party for an EIN. The IRS provides them free, instantly online, at the IRS.gov website. Some formation services charge $50-$150 for "EIN filing assistance" — this is pure markup for a free 5-minute process.

### 5. Choose the Right State (Save $200-$600/year)
Form in your home state unless you have a specific legal reason to form elsewhere.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the cheapest state to form an LLC?

By filing fee alone: Montana ($35), Kentucky ($40), and several states at $50 (Arizona, Colorado, Hawaii, Iowa, Michigan, Mississippi, Missouri, New Mexico). But total cost includes annual fees — Arizona and Mississippi have no annual reports, making them the cheapest overall. However, if you don't operate in those states, you'll pay foreign qualification fees on top.

### How long does LLC formation take?

Standard processing: 1-4 weeks in most states. Expedited: 1-3 business days ($50-$100 extra). Some states offer same-day online processing (Colorado, Wyoming, others). Online formation services typically get filings submitted within 1-2 business days.

### Can I form an LLC for free?

The state filing fee cannot be avoided. However, everything else CAN be free: self-serve as registered agent, use a free operating agreement template, get your EIN free from the IRS. Some formation services (ZenBusiness, Incfile) offer $0 basic packages where you only pay the state fee.

### Do I need a lawyer to form an LLC?

For a simple single-member LLC: no. The process is straightforward — file Articles of Organization, get an EIN, draft an operating agreement. For multi-member LLCs, LLCs in regulated industries, or LLCs planning to elect S-Corp status, attorney consultation is recommended.

### What's the difference between formation fee and franchise tax?

The formation fee is a one-time payment to create the LLC. A franchise tax is an annual fee for the privilege of operating as an LLC in the state. Not all states have franchise taxes — California ($800/year), Delaware ($300/year), and Texas (based on revenue) are the most notable.
