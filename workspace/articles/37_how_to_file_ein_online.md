## The Free Method: IRS Online Application (Recommended)

### Requirements
- Valid SSN or ITIN (for the responsible party)
- US-based applicant
- Business already formed (LLC/Corp) OR sole proprietor
- Available Monday-Friday, 7AM-10PM Eastern

### Step-by-Step Process (5-10 Minutes)

**Step 1:** Navigate to the IRS EIN Assistant (search "IRS apply for EIN online")

**Step 2:** Click "Apply Online Now" → "Begin Application"

**Step 3:** Select your entity type:
- Sole Proprietor/Individual
- Limited Liability Company (LLC)
- Corporation
- Partnership
- Trust/Estate
- Other

**Step 4:** Answer follow-up questions:
- Number of members (for LLC)
- State of formation
- Why you need an EIN (started new business, banking, etc.)

**Step 5:** Enter the responsible party's information:
- Legal name
- SSN or ITIN
- Title/role in the entity

**Step 6:** Enter business details:
- Legal name (exactly as on formation documents)
- Trade name/DBA (if different)
- Business address
- County and state
- Start date
- Closing month of accounting year
- Type of business activity
- Expected number of employees (next 12 months)

**Step 7:** Review and submit

**Step 8:** Receive your EIN immediately on screen. Print or save the confirmation.

**Total time: 5-10 minutes. Total cost: $0.**

## Common EIN Application Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Applying Before LLC Is Formed
The IRS requires your entity's exact legal name and formation date. Apply AFTER you receive confirmation that your LLC/Corp is officially filed with the state.

### Mistake 2: Entering the Wrong Entity Type
Selecting "Sole Proprietor" when you have an LLC, or vice versa, creates tax filing confusion later. Match your entity type exactly.

### Mistake 3: Using a DBA Instead of Legal Name
The "legal name" field must match your formation documents exactly (e.g., "Smith Consulting LLC" — not "Smith Consulting").

### Mistake 4: Paying for a Free Service
The IRS charges $0. Any fee you pay goes to a third party for submitting the same free application. Only worth it if bundled with other services you need.

### Mistake 5: Not Saving the Confirmation
If you close the browser without saving, you'll need to call the IRS to retrieve your EIN (long hold times). Always print or screenshot the confirmation page.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is an EIN really free?
Yes — 100% free from the IRS. Any charge is a third party's service fee, not an IRS fee.

### How fast do I get my EIN?
Online: instantly (displayed on screen upon completion). Fax: 4 business days. Mail: 4-6 weeks. Phone: during the call.

### Can I get an EIN without an SSN?
Only if you have an ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number). The IRS requires the responsible party to have either an SSN or ITIN. Foreign applicants without either must apply via fax or phone.

### Do I need a new EIN if I change my business name?
No — name changes don't require a new EIN. Inform the IRS by filing your next tax return under the new name with the same EIN, or send a letter to the IRS.

### Can a single-member LLC use the owner's SSN instead?
Technically yes (for tax purposes), but using an EIN is strongly recommended. It protects your SSN from being on every invoice, W-9, and vendor form.
