## What Is an Uncontested Divorce?

An uncontested divorce means both spouses agree on ALL major terms:

- Division of property and debts
- Child custody and visitation schedule
- Child support amount
- Spousal support (alimony) — whether any, and how much
- Who keeps the marital home
- Division of retirement accounts

**If you agree on everything:** Uncontested divorce ($300-$1,000 total)
**If you disagree on anything major:** Contested divorce ($5,000-$50,000+)

## The Online Uncontested Divorce Process (Step-by-Step)

### Step 1: Verify You Qualify

**Requirements for uncontested divorce:**
- [ ] Both spouses agree on ALL terms
- [ ] You meet your state's residency requirement (30 days to 12 months)
- [ ] You know your spouse's current address (for service)
- [ ] Neither party contests the divorce

**Additional considerations:**
- Some states require a separation period before filing (6-12 months)
- Some states require completion of a parenting course (if children involved)
- Some states have mandatory waiting periods after filing (20-180 days)

### Step 3: File with the Court

**Filing options:**
- In person at the county courthouse clerk's office
- By mail (some states)
- E-filing (available in many states — check yours)

**What to file:**
- Petition for Dissolution of Marriage (or Complaint for Divorce)
- Marital Settlement Agreement (property division terms)
- Parenting Plan (if children — custody and support details)
- Financial disclosures (some states require)
- Filing fee payment

### Step 4: Serve Your Spouse

Your spouse must be formally notified of the filing. In uncontested cases:

**Acceptance of Service / Waiver:** Your spouse signs a form acknowledging they received the papers and waive formal service. (Fastest — no process server needed)

**If spouse won't sign waiver:** You'll need formal service (process server or certified mail).

### Step 5: Wait Out the Mandatory Period

Most states require a waiting period between filing and finalization:
- **No waiting period:** Alaska, South Dakota, Washington (after filing)
- **20-30 days:** Florida, Idaho, Montana, Wyoming
- **60-90 days:** Colorado, Indiana, Kansas, Michigan, Ohio, Oklahoma, Texas
- **6 months:** California
- **12 months separation:** Maryland, North Carolina, Virginia

### Step 6: Attend Final Hearing (If Required)

Some states require a brief court appearance (5-15 minutes):
- Judge reviews your agreement
- Confirms both parties consent
- Asks if terms are fair and voluntary
- Signs the Final Decree of Divorce

**Many states waive the hearing** for fully uncontested cases — the judge signs off on paperwork without requiring your presence.

### Step 7: Receive Your Final Decree

The court issues your Decree of Divorce (or Judgment of Dissolution). You're officially divorced. Timeline from filing to decree: 30 days to 6+ months depending on your state's waiting period.

## When Online Divorce Works Best

**Ideal candidates:**
- Married less than 10 years
- Both spouses employed
- No minor children (simplest) OR agree on custody
- No complex assets (businesses, multiple properties, significant investments)
- No domestic violence
- Both spouses cooperative and communicative

**Consider attorney assistance for:**
- Significant retirement accounts requiring QDRO
- Business ownership or valuation disputes
- Real estate in multiple states
- Prenuptial agreement interpretation
- One spouse significantly out-earning the other
- Any disagreement on major terms

## State-Specific Online Divorce Notes

### California
- 6-month mandatory waiting period (longest in the US)
- Financial disclosures required (Preliminary and Final)
- Summary dissolution available for short marriages with limited assets

### Texas
- 60-day waiting period
- No-fault state — "insupportability" is sufficient grounds
- Informal ("kitchen table") agreement allowed

### Florida
- 20-day waiting period (shortest after filing)
- Financial affidavit required
- Parenting course mandatory if children involved

### New York
- No waiting period after filing
- Requires "irretrievable breakdown" for 6+ months OR separation agreement
- Index number fee + filing fee combined

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I file for divorce online without my spouse knowing?

No — your spouse must be served (notified) of the divorce filing. This is a constitutional due process requirement. However, if your spouse has disappeared, courts allow "service by publication" (newspaper notice) after documented attempts to locate them.

### How long does an online uncontested divorce take?

From start to final decree: 30 days to 7 months depending on your state's waiting period. The document preparation takes 1-2 hours. Filing and processing adds 1-4 weeks. The mandatory waiting period is usually the longest part.

### What if my spouse won't cooperate?

If your spouse won't respond to the filing, you may be able to obtain a "default divorce" — the court grants the divorce on your terms because the other party didn't respond. However, this moves from "uncontested" to a different procedure.

### Do I have to go to court?

In many states, no — the judge reviews paperwork and signs without requiring your presence (especially with COVID-era remote procedures still in place). Some states require a brief hearing.

### Is an online divorce legally valid?

Yes — the divorce decree from an online-prepared uncontested divorce is issued by the same court and has the same legal force as an attorney-handled divorce. The documents are identical; only the preparation method differs.
