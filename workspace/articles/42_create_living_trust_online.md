## How to Create Your Trust Online (Step-by-Step)

### Step 1: Choose Individual or Shared Trust
- **Individual trust:** One person (single, divorced, widowed)
- **Shared/joint trust:** Married couple (joint assets in one trust)

### Step 2: Complete the Questionnaire (30-60 minutes)
Answer questions about:
- Your assets (real estate, accounts, investments)
- Your beneficiaries (who gets what)
- Your successor trustee (who manages if you're incapacitated/deceased)
- Distribution instructions (outright, at certain ages, conditions)
- Special provisions (pet trusts, charitable gifts, spendthrift clauses)

### Step 3: Review Generated Documents
Your package typically includes:
- Revocable Living Trust document
- Pour-over Will (catches unfunded assets)
- Certificate of Trust (for banks and title companies)
- Asset schedule (list of trust property)
- Assignment of personal property to trust

### Step 4: Sign with Proper Formalities
- Trust: Sign and notarize (most states)
- Pour-over will: Sign with 2 witnesses + notarize
- Keep originals in a secure location

### Step 5: Fund Your Trust (CRITICAL)
The trust only avoids probate for assets actually transferred INTO it:
- Real estate: deed to "[Your Name], Trustee of [Trust Name]"
- Bank accounts: retitle to trust name
- Investments: transfer to trust account
- Life insurance: consider trust as beneficiary or owner

**An unfunded trust is useless.** This is the step most people skip — and why their heirs still end up in probate court.

## Who Should Use an Online Trust Service?

### Good Candidates
- Straightforward estate (home, bank accounts, retirement)
- Clear beneficiaries (children, spouse, family)
- Comfortable with self-directed legal documents
- Estate under $2 million
- No complex tax planning needed

### Hire an Attorney Instead
- Blended families with children from multiple marriages
- Special needs beneficiaries (requires SNT)
- Significant business interests
- Estate tax planning needed (estates above $7M+)
- Properties in multiple states with complex titling
- Charitable remainder trusts or other advanced strategies

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is an online living trust legally valid?
Yes — if properly signed, notarized, and funded. The legal validity comes from the document's content and execution, not who prepared it.

### Do I still need a will if I have a trust?
Yes — a "pour-over will" catches any assets not transferred to the trust and names guardians for minor children (trusts can't do this).

### How long does it take to create a trust online?
Document creation: 30-60 minutes. Funding the trust (transferring assets): days to weeks depending on your assets.

### Can I change my online trust later?
Yes — revocable trusts can be amended or revoked at any time. Most online services offer amendment documents.
