## How to Register a DBA Online (2026)

A DBA ("Doing Business As") lets you operate under a name different from your legal name or entity name — without forming a new business. Filing takes 10-30 minutes online and costs $10-$100 in most states. Here's the complete process with direct links to each state's filing portal.

## What Is a DBA and Who Needs One?

A DBA (also called "fictitious business name," "assumed name," or "trade name") is required when:

- **Sole proprietor** operating under any name other than your legal name
- **Partnership** using a name other than all partners' surnames
- **LLC or corporation** using a brand name different from the registered entity name
- **Freelancer** wanting to appear more professional (e.g., "Smith Design Studio" instead of "John Smith")

### DBA vs. LLC — Key Difference

| Feature | DBA | LLC |
|---------|-----|-----|
| Legal protection | None (no liability shield) | Personal asset protection |
| Cost | $10-$100 | $50-$500+ |
| Complexity | Simple name registration | Entity formation |
| Duration | 1-5 years (renewable) | Perpetual |
| Tax structure | Same as owner | Flexible (pass-through or corp) |
| Bank account | Yes, enables business account | Yes |
| Contracts | In owner's legal name | In LLC's name |

**Key point:** A DBA does NOT create a separate legal entity. It simply registers a business name for public record.

## How to Register a DBA Online (Step-by-Step)

### Step 1: Search for Name Availability

Before filing, confirm your desired name isn't already in use:
- Search your state's business name database
- Check the county clerk's office records (for county-level filings)
- Search the USPTO for trademark conflicts
- Google the name + your industry + your state

### Step 2: Determine WHERE to File

**State-level filing** (most common):
- California, Texas, Florida, New York, Illinois, and many others
- File with Secretary of State or equivalent agency

**County-level filing** (some states):
- File with county clerk where business operates
- May need to file in EACH county where you do business

**Both state AND county:**
- A few states require dual filing

### Step 3: Complete the Online Application

Most online DBA applications ask for:
- Your legal name (individual) or entity name (LLC/Corp)
- Desired DBA/fictitious business name
- Business address
- Type of business activity
- Owner(s) information
- Signature/attestation

### Step 4: Pay the Filing Fee

| Fee Range | States |
|:---------:|--------|
| $10-$25 | Wyoming, Montana, Idaho, South Dakota |
| $26-$50 | Texas, Florida, Ohio, Michigan, Georgia, Arizona |
| $51-$100 | California, New York, Pennsylvania, Washington |
| $100+ | Massachusetts ($150), Illinois ($150 state + county) |

### Step 5: Publish Notice (If Required)

Some states require publishing your DBA in a local newspaper:

| State | Publication Required? | Duration | Approximate Cost |
|-------|:--------------------:|:--------:|:---------------:|
| California | Yes | Once (within 30 days of filing) | $30-$200 |
| New York | Yes | 6 consecutive weeks | $200-$1,500+ |
| Illinois | Yes | 3 consecutive weeks | $50-$200 |
| Arizona | Yes | 3 consecutive weeks | $50-$150 |
| Florida | No | — | $0 |
| Texas | No | — | $0 |
| Georgia | No | — | $0 |

### Step 6: Receive Your DBA Certificate

After processing (1-5 business days for online filing), you receive:
- DBA certificate or filing receipt
- Confirmation number
- Use this to open a business bank account under your DBA name

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does DBA registration take?
Online filing: instant to 5 business days for most states. Mail filing: 1-3 weeks. If publication is required, add 3-6 weeks for the notice period.

### Can I have multiple DBAs?
Yes — you can register as many DBAs as you need. Each requires a separate filing and fee. Common for businesses operating multiple brands.

### Does a DBA protect my business name?
No — a DBA only registers your name at the state/county level. It doesn't prevent someone in another county or state from using the same name. For nationwide protection, you need a trademark.

### Do I need a DBA if I have an LLC?
Only if you want to operate under a name DIFFERENT from your LLC's registered name. If your LLC is "Smith Holdings LLC" but you want to operate as "Smith Coffee Shop," you need a DBA.

### Can I use my DBA to accept payments?
Yes — once you open a bank account under your DBA name, you can accept checks and payments made out to your business name instead of your personal name.
