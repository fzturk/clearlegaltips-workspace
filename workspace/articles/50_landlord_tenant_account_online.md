## How to Set Up Your Account (Step-by-Step)

### Step 1: Choose Your Platform

**For 1-3 rental units (start free):**
- **Avail** — Best all-around free platform
- **TurboTenant** — Unlimited units on free plan
- **Apartments.com** — Simplest interface

**For 4-20 units:**
- **Avail Premium** ($7/unit/month) — Adds features
- **Rentec Direct** ($45-$55/month) — Professional tools

**For 20+ units:**
- **Buildium** or **AppFolio** — Full property management suite

### Step 2: Create Your Landlord Account (5 minutes)

1. Go to your chosen platform's website
2. Select "Landlord" or "Property Owner" account type
3. Enter your name, email, and create a password
4. Verify your email address
5. Complete your profile (phone number, mailing address)

### Step 3: Add Your Properties (5 minutes per property)

Enter for each property:
- Property address
- Property type (single-family, multi-family, condo, apartment)
- Number of units
- Monthly rent amount
- Lease start and end dates
- Security deposit amount
- Pet policy
- Parking details

### Step 4: Set Up Rent Collection (10 minutes)

**Connect your bank account:**
- Link via Plaid (instant bank verification) or micro-deposits (2-3 days)
- ACH transfers are free on most platforms
- Funds typically arrive in 2-5 business days

**Configure payment settings:**
- Due date (typically 1st of month)
- Grace period (3-5 days common)
- Late fee amount (check state maximums)
- Auto-pay option (tenant can enable)
- Accepted payment methods (ACH, credit card, debit card)

**Late fee rules by state (selected):**

| State | Late Fee Maximum | Grace Period Required? |
|-------|:---------------:|:--------------------:|
| California | No statutory max (must be "reasonable") | No (but common) |
| Texas | 12% of rent (10% for single-family, 12% for multi-family) | Must wait until rent is late |
| Florida | No statutory max | No |
| New York | $50 or 5% of rent (whichever is less) | 5 days required |
| Illinois | No statutory max | 5 days required (Chicago) |
| Oregon | 5% of rent first late; 10% for subsequent | 4 days (8th day of month) |
| Washington | No statutory max | No |
| Pennsylvania | No statutory max | No |
| Colorado | $50 or 5% (whichever is greater) | No |

### Step 5: Upload or Create Your Lease (10 minutes)

**Options:**
- Upload your existing lease document (PDF)
- Use the platform's lease template (state-specific)
- Create a new lease from scratch using built-in tools

**Key lease clauses to include:**
- Rent amount and due date
- Late fee policy (matching your platform settings)
- Security deposit terms
- Maintenance responsibilities
- Pet policy
- Termination and renewal terms
- Online payment requirement (if mandating digital payments)

## Rent Collection: ACH vs. Credit Card vs. Check

| Method | Cost to Landlord | Cost to Tenant | Speed | Chargeback Risk |
|--------|:---------------:|:--------------:|:-----:|:--------------:|
| ACH (bank transfer) | $0 (most platforms) | $0-$1.49 | 2-5 days | Very low |
| Credit card | 2.5-3.5% of rent | $0 (landlord absorbs) or tenant pays | 1-3 days | Higher |
| Debit card | $0-1% | $0-$1.49 | 1-3 days | Low |
| Paper check | $0 | Postage + check cost | 3-7 days (mail + processing) | Bounced check risk |
| Cash | $0 | $0 | Immediate | None |

**Recommendation:** ACH is the gold standard — free, reliable, and low risk. Allow credit card as a backup option.

## Legal Considerations for Online Rent Collection

### Can You Require Online Payments?

| State/City | Can Require Online Payment? |
|-----------|:-------------------------:|
| Most states | Yes (with reasonable alternatives available) |
| New York City | No — must accept at least one in-person method |
| California | Depends — must offer reasonable alternative |
| Oregon | Must accept at least one non-electronic method |

**Best practice:** Offer online payment as the PRIMARY method but maintain one alternative (money order, cashier's check) for tenants without bank accounts.

### Security Deposit Management

Some platforms handle security deposit tracking:
- Separate escrow accounting (required in some states)
- Interest calculation (required in some states: New York, New Jersey, Maryland, Connecticut)
- Move-out inspection documentation
- Automated deposit return calculations

### Record Keeping

Online platforms automatically maintain:
- Payment history (date, amount, method)
- Communication logs
- Maintenance request history
- Lease documents and amendments
- These records are invaluable for disputes, tax audits, and eviction proceedings

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is online rent collection safe?
Yes — major platforms use bank-level encryption (256-bit SSL), are regulated by financial authorities, and process payments through established banking networks. They're safer than accepting checks (which can bounce) or cash (which is untraceable).

### What if my tenant doesn't have a bank account?
Most platforms allow credit/debit card payments as an alternative. Some tenants can use cash deposit networks (PayNearMe). You should always offer at least one non-electronic payment option.

### Can I collect rent through Venmo or Zelle?
You can, but it's NOT recommended for landlords:
- No automatic late fee tracking
- No lease integration
- Limited payment records for tax purposes
- No tenant screening or maintenance features
- Dispute resolution is limited
- Payments may be classified as personal (tax reporting complications)

### How do I handle a tenant who stops paying online?
The platform tracks non-payment automatically. You can:
1. Send automated payment reminders (platform handles this)
2. Apply late fees (automatic if configured)
3. Send formal notice through the platform
4. If needed, begin eviction process with documented payment history

### Are there tax benefits to using a rent collection platform?
The platform itself isn't tax-deductible as a personal expense, but the subscription fee (if paid) is deductible as a business expense on Schedule E. More importantly, the automatic record-keeping makes tax preparation significantly easier and reduces audit risk.
