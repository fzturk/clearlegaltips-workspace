## How to Dissolve an LLC Online (2026)

Closing an LLC isn't as simple as stopping operations. You must formally dissolve with the state, settle debts, file final tax returns, and cancel registrations — or you'll continue owing annual fees, franchise taxes, and face potential penalties. Here's the complete dissolution process, state by state.

## Why You Must Formally Dissolve (Don't Just Walk Away)

**What happens if you DON'T dissolve:**
- Continue owing annual report fees ($50-$800/year depending on state)
- Continue owing franchise taxes (California: $800/year minimum)
- Late fees and penalties accumulate
- State eventually "administratively dissolves" your LLC — but with penalties attached
- Your registered agent continues billing you
- Credit damage from unpaid state obligations
- Personal liability risk if the LLC is used fraudulently by others

**Formal dissolution costs:** $0-$100 filing fee (one time)
**Ignoring dissolution costs:** $500-$5,000+ in accumulated fees and penalties

## Step-by-Step LLC Dissolution Process

### Step 1: Vote to Dissolve

Your operating agreement should specify how dissolution decisions are made:
- **Single-member LLC:** Member decides unilaterally
- **Multi-member LLC:** Usually requires majority or unanimous vote
- **No operating agreement:** Default state rules apply (typically majority)

**Document the vote:** Write a resolution recording the decision to dissolve, signed by all consenting members.

### Step 2: File Articles of Dissolution (or Certificate of Cancellation)

File with the same state agency where you formed your LLC (usually Secretary of State).

**Information required:**
- LLC name and state filing number
- Date of dissolution
- Reason for dissolution
- Confirmation debts are settled (or plan for settling)
- Signature of authorized member/manager

### Step 3: Notify Creditors

Most states require you to:
- Send written notice to known creditors
- Publish notice to unknown creditors (some states)
- Give creditors 90-120 days to submit claims
- Pay valid claims before distributing assets to members

### Step 4: File Final Tax Returns

**Federal:**
- Final Form 1065 (multi-member) or Schedule C (single-member)
- Check "Final Return" box
- File by standard deadline for the tax year of dissolution

**State:**
- Final state income/franchise tax return
- California: File Form 568 (final) + pay $800 franchise tax for dissolution year
- Texas: File final Public Information Report + franchise tax report
- New York: File final IT-204

**Employment taxes (if you had employees):**
- Final Form 941 (quarterly payroll tax)
- Final Form 940 (annual FUTA)
- Final W-2s and W-3

### Step 5: Cancel Registrations and Licenses

- Business licenses (city/county)
- Sales tax permits
- Professional licenses
- DBA registrations
- Foreign qualifications in other states
- State-specific registrations (e.g., employer registration)

### Step 6: Close Accounts and Distribute Assets

- Close business bank accounts (after all checks clear)
- Close business credit cards
- Cancel insurance policies
- Distribute remaining assets per operating agreement (or equally if no agreement)
- Cancel registered agent service (AFTER dissolution is filed)

## Special State Considerations

### California
- Must file Certificate of Cancellation with Secretary of State ($0)
- Must file final Form 568 AND pay $800 minimum franchise tax for dissolution year
- If you owe back franchise taxes, pay ALL before cancellation is accepted
- Tax clearance NOT required (unlike corporations)

### Texas
- File Certificate of Termination online via SOSDirect ($40)
- File final franchise tax report with Comptroller
- Must include Tax ID number on termination filing
- Consider filing a final Public Information Report

### New York
- File Articles of Dissolution ($60) online
- Must publish dissolution notice (some counties — verify)
- File final state tax return
- Request a tax clearance letter from Department of Taxation (optional but recommended)

### Delaware
- File Certificate of Cancellation ($200)
- Must be current on all annual taxes ($300/year minimum)
- Pay any outstanding franchise taxes before filing
- Online filing through Delaware Division of Corporations

### Florida
- File Articles of Dissolution online via Sunbiz ($25)
- No state income tax — simplifies tax obligations
- Must file final annual report for the dissolution year ($138.75)
- Cancel sales tax registration with Department of Revenue

## Common Dissolution Mistakes

1. **Not filing at all** — Accumulated fees and penalties
2. **Forgetting final tax returns** — IRS penalties
3. **Not canceling foreign qualifications** — Still owe fees in other states
4. **Distributing assets before paying creditors** — Personal liability
5. **Canceling registered agent before filing dissolution** — Miss important notices
6. **Not notifying creditors** — Claims against members personally
7. **Forgetting California franchise tax** — $800 for the year you dissolve, even if no revenue

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does LLC dissolution take?
Filing processing: 1-5 business days (online) or 2-4 weeks (mail). Full dissolution process including creditor notification and tax filings: 60-120 days recommended.

### Can I dissolve an LLC with outstanding debts?
You must address all debts before dissolution. Options: pay in full, negotiate settlements, or have the LLC declare bankruptcy (rare for small LLCs). Members may become personally liable for debts if LLC is dissolved without paying creditors.

### What about my EIN after dissolution?
EINs cannot be "canceled" — they're permanent. But you can close the business account associated with the EIN by sending a letter to the IRS. The EIN remains assigned to your LLC forever.

### Can I reuse my LLC name after dissolution?
In most states, the name becomes available again after dissolution is processed. However, someone else could register it. If you want to preserve the name, consider keeping the LLC active (just inactive commercially).

### Do I still need to file taxes for the year I dissolve?
Yes — file final returns for the portion of the year the LLC operated. Mark all returns as "FINAL." This includes federal, state, and any local returns.
