## Types of Business Licenses

Not all "business licenses" are the same. Most businesses need some combination of the following:

### 1. General Business License (City/County)
Most cities and counties require a general business license (also called a business tax certificate, business operating permit, or business registration) simply to operate within their jurisdiction.

**Cost range:** $25-$500/year
**Renewal:** Annual in most jurisdictions

### 2. State Business License
Some states require a statewide business license in addition to (or instead of) local permits.

**States requiring a state-level general business license:**
- Nevada: $200/year (Business License)
- Washington: $19-$90 (Business License Application)
- Delaware: $75 (Business License for LLC)
- Alaska: $25-$2,000+ (depends on industry)

**States with NO general state business license:** Most states — they rely on entity registration (LLC/Corp filing) plus industry-specific licensing.

## How to Determine Your License Requirements

### Step 1: Check State Requirements
Search "[Your State] + business license requirements" at your Secretary of State or Department of Revenue website. Some states have a centralized license search tool.

### Step 2: Check City/County Requirements
Contact your city clerk or county clerk's office. Most cities publish license requirements on their website. Search "[Your City] + business license application."

### Step 3: Check Industry-Specific Requirements
Professional and regulated industries require additional licenses from specific state boards. Search "[Your Profession] + license + [Your State]."

### Step 4: Check Federal Requirements
Some industries require federal licenses or permits:
- Alcohol (TTB)
- Firearms (ATF)
- Aviation (FAA)
- Broadcasting (FCC)
- Transportation (DOT/FMCSA)
- Agriculture (USDA)
- Import/Export (CBP)

## Cost Reduction Strategies

### Home-Based Business Exemptions
Many cities offer reduced fees or exemptions for home-based businesses. A home occupation permit typically costs $0-$150 compared to $200-$500 for a commercial business license.

### New Business Discounts
Some cities prorate license fees for businesses starting mid-year. Others offer first-year discounts or fee waivers for new businesses.

### Online/No-Location Business
If you operate entirely online with no physical presence in a city, you may not need a local business license from that city. You still need licenses for the city where YOU are physically located.

## Common Business License Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Assuming You Don't Need One
Most cities require a general business license — even for home-based, online, or freelance businesses. Operating without one can result in fines ($100-$1,000+) and back fees.

### Mistake 2: Getting Only the State License
State registration (LLC/Corp filing) is NOT the same as a business license. Most businesses need both entity registration AND local business licenses/permits.

### Mistake 3: Forgetting to Renew
Business licenses expire annually in most jurisdictions. Set a calendar reminder. Operating on an expired license can result in penalties and inability to enforce contracts.

### Mistake 4: Not Checking All Jurisdictions
If you have a physical office in one city but deliver services in another, you may need licenses in BOTH cities. Multi-location businesses need licenses for each location.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do online businesses need a business license?

In most cases, yes. You need a business license for the city/county where YOU are physically located (even if your customers are everywhere). You may also need licenses in cities where you have employees, warehouses, or significant operations.

### How long does it take to get a business license?

General business licenses: typically 1-4 weeks. Professional licenses: 2-12 weeks (depending on background checks, exams, experience verification). Some cities offer same-day processing for general licenses.

### Can I operate while my license application is pending?

Policies vary by jurisdiction. Some cities issue a temporary permit upon application. Others require you to wait for approval. Never assume you can operate without checking.

### What's the penalty for operating without a business license?

Varies by jurisdiction: $100-$1,000+ in fines per violation, back fees for all unlicensed periods, potential criminal misdemeanor charges in some jurisdictions, and inability to enforce contracts or collect on accounts.
