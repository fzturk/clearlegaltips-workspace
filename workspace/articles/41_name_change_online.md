## How to File a Legal Name Change Online (2026)

Whether you're changing your name after marriage, divorce, personal preference, or for your child — the legal process involves court paperwork, a filing fee, and (in most states) a brief court hearing. Some states now allow online filing; all allow online document preparation. Here's the complete process.

## Adult Name Change Process (Step-by-Step)

### Step 1: Prepare Your Petition
File a "Petition for Change of Name" with your county court. The petition includes:
- Current legal name
- Requested new name
- Reason for the change
- Statement that the change is NOT for fraudulent purposes
- Criminal history disclosure (required in most states)

### Step 2: File and Pay
- Filing fee: $150-$500 (varies by state/county)
- File with: County Superior Court / Circuit Court / District Court

### Step 3: Publish Notice (If Required)
Many states require publishing your name change in a local newspaper for 1-4 weeks. Cost: $30-$200.

**States that may waive publication:** Domestic violence victims, transgender individuals (varies by jurisdiction), certain safety exceptions.

### Step 4: Attend Court Hearing
- Brief hearing (5-15 minutes)
- Judge confirms no fraudulent purpose
- No objections filed
- Judge signs the order

### Step 5: Update Your Documents
After receiving the court order:
- Social Security card (free — SSA office)
- Driver's license ($10-$50)
- Passport ($130-$160)
- Bank accounts (free)
- Credit cards, utilities, subscriptions

## Minor Child Name Change

Changing a child's name requires BOTH parents' consent (or court override):
- Both parents sign the petition — straightforward
- One parent objects — hearing required; judge decides based on child's best interest
- Absent parent — court may allow name change after documented attempts to notify

**Additional requirements for minors:**
- Both parents named in petition
- Child's consent (if over 12-14 in many states)
- Best interest of the child standard applies

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I change my name to anything?
Almost — with restrictions. Courts reject names that: are intended to defraud, contain obscenity, consist only of numerals (in some states), or are trademarked names intended to mislead.

### How long does a name change take?
Filing to court order: 4-12 weeks. Updating all documents afterward: 2-4 additional weeks. Total: 6-16 weeks.

### Do I need a lawyer for a name change?
For straightforward adult name changes: usually no. The forms are relatively simple. For contested minor name changes or criminal history complications: attorney recommended.

### Is a name change after divorce automatic?
In most states, you can request your maiden name restoration in the divorce decree itself — no separate petition needed. If you didn't request it during divorce, you'll need a separate name change petition.
