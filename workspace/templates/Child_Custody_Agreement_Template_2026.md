## 1. LEGAL CUSTODY (Decision-Making Authority)

**Legal custody determines who makes major decisions about the children.**

**[ ] Joint Legal Custody (Recommended — Most Common)**
Both parents share decision-making authority for major decisions including:
- Education (school enrollment, tutoring, special education)
- Healthcare (medical, dental, mental health, medications)
- Religious upbringing
- Extracurricular activities
- Travel outside the country

**[ ] Sole Legal Custody to [PARENT NAME]**
[PARENT NAME] has sole authority for all major decisions. The other parent shall be informed of decisions but does not have veto power.

**Decision-Making Process (if Joint):**
(a) Both parents shall discuss major decisions and attempt to reach agreement;
(b) If unable to agree within [7 / 14] days, the parents shall [attend mediation / Parent [1/2] shall have final decision on [CATEGORY]];
(c) In a medical emergency, the parent with the child may make immediate necessary decisions and inform the other parent as soon as possible.

## 6. RELOCATION

**6.1** Neither parent shall relocate with the children more than [25 / 50 / 100] miles from their current address without:
(a) [60 / 90] days' written notice to the other parent; AND

**6.2** Notice shall include: new address, reason for move, proposed revised parenting schedule.

## 8. PARENTING GUIDELINES

Both parents agree to:
(a) Not disparage the other parent in the children's presence;
(b) Not discuss legal/financial disputes in front of the children;
(c) Not use the children as messengers between parents;
(d) Support the children's relationship with both parents;
(e) Give [48 hours / 1 week] notice of schedule changes when possible;
(f) Offer the other parent right of first refusal if unable to care for the children for more than [4 / 8 / 24] hours during their custodial time;
(g) Not introduce new romantic partners to the children until the relationship is [3 / 6] months old (or [there are no restrictions]);
(h) Maintain consistent rules about [bedtime / screen time / homework] at both homes (or [each household sets its own rules]).

---

## 9. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

If parents disagree on any matter:
1. Discuss directly (in person or by phone)
2. If unresolved: mediation with [NAMED MEDIATOR / court-appointed mediator]
3. If still unresolved: petition the court

**Neither parent shall withhold visitation as a remedy for disputes.**

---

## 10. MODIFICATIONS

This Agreement may be modified by:
(a) Written agreement of both parents (filed with the court for enforceability); OR
(b) Court order upon petition by either parent showing a material change in circumstances.
