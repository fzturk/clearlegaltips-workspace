## 4. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR STATUS

**4.1** Consultant is an independent contractor, NOT an employee of Client. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employer-employee relationship, partnership, or joint venture.

**4.2** Consultant shall:
(a) Control the manner and means of performing Services;
(b) Set their own schedule (subject to reasonable deadlines);
(c) Provide their own tools, equipment, and workspace;
(d) Be free to perform services for other clients (subject to confidentiality and non-compete);
(e) Not receive employee benefits (health insurance, retirement, paid leave, etc.);
(f) Be solely responsible for all taxes, including self-employment tax.

**4.3 Tax Obligations:**
- Consultant is responsible for paying all federal, state, and local taxes on compensation received.
- Client will issue Form 1099-NEC for payments of $600 or more in a calendar year.
- Consultant's Tax ID (EIN or SSN): [PROVIDED SEPARATELY]

## 6. CONFIDENTIALITY

**6.1 Confidential Information:** Each party agrees to keep confidential all non-public information received from the other party, including but not limited to:
- Business strategies, plans, and financial information
- Customer lists and data
- Trade secrets and proprietary processes
- Technical data, source code, and algorithms
- Marketing strategies and pricing
- Employee and contractor information

**6.2 Exclusions:** Confidentiality obligations do not apply to information that:
(a) Is or becomes publicly available (not through breach);
(b) Was known to the receiving party before disclosure;
(c) Is independently developed without reference to confidential information;
(d) Is received from a third party without restriction.

**6.3 Duration:** Confidentiality obligations survive for [2 / 3 / 5] years after termination of this Agreement. Trade secrets remain protected indefinitely.

**6.4 Return of Materials:** Upon termination, each party shall return or destroy all confidential materials and certify destruction in writing upon request.

(c) Work product will be original and will not infringe third-party IP rights;
(d) Consultant has no conflicting obligations that would prevent performance;

**9.2 Client represents and warrants that:**

(b) Client will provide necessary access, information, and cooperation;
(c) Client-provided materials do not infringe third-party rights.

**10.2** NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, REGARDLESS OF CAUSE.

## 11. INDEMNIFICATION

**11.1** Consultant shall indemnify Client against third-party claims arising from:
(a) Consultant's negligence or willful misconduct;
(b) Intellectual property infringement in deliverables (excluding Client-provided materials);
(c) Consultant's breach of this Agreement;
(d) Tax liabilities related to Consultant's independent contractor status.

**11.2** Client shall indemnify Consultant against third-party claims arising from:
(a) Client's use of deliverables outside the intended scope;
(b) Client-provided materials that infringe third-party rights;
(c) Client's negligence or willful misconduct.

Either party may terminate with [14 / 30] days' written notice.

**12.2 Termination for Cause:**
Either party may terminate immediately if the other party:
(a) Materially breaches and fails to cure within [15] days of written notice;
(b) Becomes insolvent or files for bankruptcy;
(c) Engages in fraud, illegal activity, or gross misconduct.

**12.3 Effect of Termination:**
(a) Client pays for all Services completed through termination date;
(b) Consultant delivers all completed and in-progress work product;
(c) If Client terminates for convenience: [Consultant keeps any non-refundable deposit / Client pays a kill fee of $[AMOUNT] / [__]% of remaining project fee];

## 15. GENERAL PROVISIONS

**(a) Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, including all exhibits, constitutes the entire agreement. Supersedes all prior discussions and agreements.

**(b) Amendments.** Must be in writing signed by both parties.

**(c) Assignment.** Consultant may not assign without Client's written consent. Client may assign to a successor entity.

**(d) Notices.** Written notices sent to addresses above, effective upon receipt.

**(e) Force Majeure.** Neither party liable for delays due to events beyond reasonable control (natural disasters, pandemics, government actions).

**(f) Severability.** Invalid provisions shall be reformed to minimum extent necessary; remaining provisions continue.

**(g) Governing Law.** Laws of the State of **[STATE]**.

**(h) Counterparts.** May be signed in counterparts, including electronic signatures.
