# Brand Voice — ClearLegalTips

## Voice Profile

**Who we are:** A practical legal guidance resource for US adults who want to handle common legal tasks themselves, without paying a lawyer for every step.

**Tone:** Clear + Professional + Empathetic  
**NOT:** Academic, alarmist, salesy, condescending, or overly casual

**Reader mental model:** "I'm not a lawyer. I need to understand this. I need to know what to do. Don't talk down to me."

## Core Voice Rules

### ✅ DO

- **Use "you"** — Write directly to the reader. "You can file this online" not "One may file this online."
- **Answer first** — Lead with the answer, then explain. "Yes, you need an operating agreement. Here's why..."
- **Plain English** — Replace legal jargon with plain terms. First use: define the term. "An LLC operating agreement (a document that outlines how your business is run) should..."
- **Hedge appropriately** — Use "in most states", "generally", "typically" to avoid giving absolute legal advice.
- **Short sentences** — Average 15–20 words. Break anything over 30 words into two sentences.
- **Active voice** — "File your articles of organization" not "Articles of organization should be filed."
- **Empathy signals** — Acknowledge the reader's situation: "This can feel overwhelming, but..."

### ❌ DON'T

- **No legal advice absolutes** — Never "You must", "You are required to" without "in most states" or similar qualifier.
- **No fear-mongering** — Don't exaggerate consequences to push affiliate clicks.
- **No fluff openers** — Never "In today's fast-paced world..." or "Have you ever wondered..."
- **No excessive affiliate pressure** — Max 2–3 CTA buttons per article. Never claim a service is "the best" without evidence.
- **No ALL CAPS for emphasis** — Use **bold** instead.
- **No em-dash overuse** — Maximum 1–2 per article.
- **No "simply" or "just"** — These minimize the reader's difficulty.

## Vocabulary Guide

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|---|---|
| Utilize | Use |
| Commence | Start / Begin |
| Terminate | End |
| Endeavor | Try |

| Subsequent to | After |
| In lieu of | Instead of |

## Legal Disclaimer Tone

Every article ends with the disclaimer. The disclaimer should not undermine the article — frame it as context, not a caveat:

✅ "The information here is for general guidance. Your state's rules may differ — always confirm with official state resources or a licensed attorney for your specific situation."

❌ "Nothing in this article constitutes legal advice and you should not rely on it for any legal matter."
