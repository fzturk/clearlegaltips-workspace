# Case Study Builder

## Context

E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) is critical for legal content ranking. Google applies heightened scrutiny to YMYL (Your Money or Your Life) topics — legal content qualifies. Case studies demonstrating real outcomes boost E-E-A-T signals and build reader trust before affiliate clicks.

- David Miller (ID 294) — small business, LLC formation

## Case Study Types

### 1. Reader Outcome Story
"How [Name] formed an LLC in [State] in [timeframe] using [service]"
- Specific, concrete details
- Before/after contrast
- Cost breakdown (what they paid vs. hiring a lawyer)
- Affiliate service featured naturally

### 2. DIY vs. Lawyer Comparison
"Why [Name] chose to file their own NDA instead of hiring an attorney"
- Concrete savings ($500–$2,000 typical)
- Process comparison
- When DIY is appropriate vs. when to hire

### 3. Common Mistake Avoided
"The LLC mistake that would have cost [Name] $3,000 — and how a $49 service prevented it"
- Specific legal risk identified
- How the service/template solved it
- Prevention angle (high E-E-A-T trust signal)

### 4. State-Specific Success
"How [Name] navigated [State]'s unusual LLC requirements without a lawyer"
- Highlights state-specific complexity
- Shows reader the value of guidance
- Natural affiliate CTA for registered agent / formation service

## Instructions

1. **Determine case study type** — outcome story / comparison / mistake avoided / state-specific
2. **Create a realistic but anonymized subject** — use first name only ("Michael from Texas"), specific enough to feel real
3. **Define the specific legal task** — align with target affiliate
4. **Build the narrative** using the structure below
5. **Embed the affiliate CTA naturally** — the service is part of the solution, not a bolt-on
6. **Include cost specifics** — readers trust specific numbers over vague claims

## Case Study Structure

```
Headline: How [Name] [Achieved Outcome] in [Timeframe] (Without a Lawyer)

The Situation:
[2–3 sentences setting up the problem. Specific state, specific document needed, specific trigger event.]

The Options:
[2–3 sentences covering their choices: hire a lawyer ($X–$X), DIY completely free (risks), use an online service ($X–$X).]

The Decision:
[1–2 sentences on which path they chose and why — cost, speed, simplicity.]

The Process:
[3–5 steps they actually took. Specific actions. Reference the service used.]

The Outcome:
[Concrete result — document signed, LLC approved, landlord dispute resolved. Timeframe. Total cost.]

What They Saved:
[Cost comparison: "A business attorney would have charged $800–$1,500 for this. Total cost: $49."]

The Takeaway:
[1–2 sentences on what other readers in the same situation should know.]

[Affiliate CTA]
```

## E-E-A-T Signals to Include

- [ ] Specific state mentioned
- [ ] Specific dollar amounts (savings, costs)
- [ ] Specific timeframe (2 hours, 3 business days)
- [ ] Real legal terminology used correctly
- [ ] Outcome that can be verified (LLC approved by state, NDA signed, lease executed)
- [ ] Author byline from relevant expert (e.g., Marcus Thorne for LLC case studies)
